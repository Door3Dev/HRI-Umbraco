  • About Us

Health Republic Insurance of New York is a true not-for-profit program and New York’s only CO-OP -- Consumer Operated and Oriented Plan.  CO-OPs are private, member-governed health insurance companies forming across the country as part of the Affordable Care Act.  Health Republic Insurance of New York is one of 23 CO-OPs nationally.

Not-for-Profit.  For People.

Just as people come together to form credit unions, electric, food and housing coops, our CO-OP members join together to invest all profits back into a better system.  Health Republic has three primary goals:

  1. reduce health care costs
  2. make health insurance accessible to millions of people
  3. give individuals a voice in their health care

 

We’re Different.

Health Republic Insurance of New York’s benefit packages are different and our membership experience is different.  Here’s just a few of our special features:

  • We have the lowest-priced health insurance option in most of the markets we serve.
  • With our PrimarySelect plans, when you choose a Primary Physician, there are no-copays or deductibles on visits to that physician.
  • We have no gatekeepers – you need no referral to see any provider in our network.
  • We have Wellness Navigators standing by to help our members make the most of their benefits and find community resources to stay healthy.
  • Our members have access to more than 40 types of alternative care and fitness providers, all providing significant discounts on their services.
  • Our members will vote for the leadership of our organization.

 

We’re The Same.

At the same time, we are held to the same high standards for all New York health insurance organizations:

  • All health insurance plans in New York must cover the same set of Essential Health Benefits. These include medical care, medications, mental health care, and a host of other services.
  • Our EssentialCare plans meet all conditions for the New York State “standard” plan, allowing consumers to make an apples-to-apples comparison.
  • Our benefit plans, premiums and provider network coverage are all approved and monitored by the New York State Department of Financial Services.
