    WE’RE HERE TO HELP

    At Health Republic, we're committed to providing members and prospective members with the information they need to get the most out of their plans.

    Please contact us with any questions or comments in one of the two ways below:

    •         Email: questions@newyork.healthrepublic.us. Be sure to provide your name, address, and telephone number and we will respond as quickly as possible.

    •         Call: 888-990-5702, Monday through Friday. 8:30 am to 5:30 pm EST

    We also encourage you to review our FAQs to learn more.

     

     

    Enrollment

  • [ + ]Who can get health insurance through Health Republic Insurance of New York?

    Anyone who lives in New York in our service areas can buy insurance coverage through Health Republic. Our services areas are New York City, Long Island, Hudson Valley and surrounding area, The Capital District and parts of North County, Parts of Central and Western New York. You can learn more by attending a community meeting led by our Ambassadors throughout these service areas.

  • [ + ]When can I enroll?

    Enrollment begins October 1, 2013 and ends March 31, 2014. You can enroll through the nystateofhealth.ny.gov Exchange, through an insurance broker, or by calling 888-990-5702. We also have Ambassadors in your community who can help you, so call us and let us know.

  • [ + ]Can you make a special price for our group?

    If you would like a custom plan for your employee group of more than 50, the answer is Yes, we can discuss that -- please call our main number at 888-990-5702, and select the option for "Brokers." But the small group plans we offer cannot be modified until 2015.

  • [ + ]When do I receive my member card?

    Our systems are being put together, and you will get your card in January, 2014.

  • [ + ]How do I pay my monthly premium?

    You may pay for your coverage in one of the following ways:

    • Make online payments from your bank account, or
    • Sign up for automatic monthly withdrawals from your bank account, or
    • Send a check or money order to the address indicated on the enclosed statement.

    • Coverage

  • [ + ]How do I find out if my favorite doctors are on Health Republic Insurance of New York?

    There are two ways – first, you can search our up-to-date provider directory. You can also ask your doctors, but please remember to say that Health Republic Insurance of New York is working with the MagnaCare network.

  • [ + ]When will my health coverage begin?

    We'll begin signing people up for health insurance when Open Enrollment starts October 1, 2013. Coverage will begin in January 1, 2014. You can still enroll in January, February, and March 2014, with coverage beginning the following month (or within 2 months if enrolling after the 15th of the month) Small and large groups may enroll throughout the year.

  • [ + ]Can I be denied coverage if I have a health condition?

    No. The Affordable Care Act prohibits all health insurance companies from rejecting anyone for a pre-existing health condition as of January 2014. Your eligibility will not be affected by your health status when you apply for insurance.

  • [ + ]How do I submit a claim for a medical reimbursement to cover expenses that I paid out of pocket?

    If you have paid out of pocket for services that are normally covered by your plan, you may be eligible for a reimbursement. To submit a claim for reimbursement, log onto your member account, print and complete the Health Benefit Claim Form under Forms & Guides and follow the instructions to submit for approval.

    • Wellness

  • [ + ]What is the Gym Membership Reimbursement?

    As a member of Health Republic Insurance of New York (HRINY), you are eligible for a reimbursement of up to $200 on gym membership fees when you go to the gym 50 times within a consecutive six-month period. In addition, if your spouse or domestic partner is also covered on your HRINY plan, he or she is also eligible for a reimbursement of up to $100 if he or she also uses the gym 50 times within a consecutive six-month period. Gym visits must occur during active HRINY coverage. Find out more by logging into your Member Account and finding the Gym Membership Reimbursement Form under “Forms & Guides.”

    To access HRINY bonus services, you must be an actively enrolled HRINY member.

  • [ + ]What is the General Health Assessment?

    This brief questionnaire provides you with a summary of your health, giving you immediate information about health concerns and health needs you may have. Once completed, you can take this summary to your physician and discuss it during your first well visit. Scheduling a well visit with your physician is the key to good health. Log in now to your HRINY Member Account and click on “questionnaire” in the General Health Assessment section to complete the assessment.

    Plus, when you complete your HRINY General Health Assessment you will be eligible for 3 free Stat Doctors e-visits, while supplies last.

    The General Health Assessment can be completed once per calander year. 

     

  • [ + ]What is Stat Doctors?

    Stat Doctors e-visits, available to all actively enrolled HRINY members, provide access to leading board certified physicians who are trained in the specialty of emergency medicine and available for on-demand care 24 hours a day 7 days a week, for a service fee of $25 per visit. However, when you complete your HRINY General Health Assessment you’ll be eligible for 3 free visits from Stat Doctors. Log in to your Member Account and complete the questionnaire now!

     For more details about Stat Doctors, visit www.StatDoctors.com or call 1-877-585-STAT.

     To access HRINY bonus services, you must be an actively enrolled HRINY member.

  • [ + ]What is the Diabetes Program?

    The Diabetes Program is a voluntary program for HRINY members with diabetes. The program provides support, education, information, as well as reminders and tips about managing diabetes. Members with complex care needs may be referred to Case Management. Diabetes supplies can also be ordered through this program. Eligible members can call Better Living Now for more information at 1-800-854-5729 or visit www.BetterLivingNow.com.

  • [ + ]Does HRINY cover Alternative Medicine?

    All HRINY members have access to discounted alternative care services through Healthways WholeHealth Living® by logging in to your Member Account. Healthways offers Complementary and Alternative Medicine (CAM) benefits to all HRINY members, with savings of up to 30% off of acupuncture, massage, spa, exercise facilities, chiropractic care and other services.

    Also, many HRINY plans include services such as acupuncture and chiropractic care. Consult your explanation of plan benefits to see what is included in your plan.

    To access HRINY bonus services, you must be an actively enrolled HRINY member.

    • Financial help available under The Affordable Care Act

  • [ + ]What kind of financial assistance is available?

    If you earn less than 400% of the Federal Poverty Level (FPL) you may be eligible for assistance from the federal government to help pay for your coverage. This is up to $45,960 for an individual and up to $96,200 for a family of four.

    There are two types of financial assistance: one is advanced premium tax credits (APTC) and the second is cost-sharing reductions (CSRs). For those who are eligible for APTCs, you have the option of having the federal government pay that amount of the premium to Health Republic directly, allowing you to pay a smaller portion of the total premium each month; alternatively, you can receive the full premium tax credit towards your 2014 taxes when you file them. For those who are also eligible for CSRs, the federal government can assist with your copayments and deductibles too.

    We recommend that you use the tax credit and subsidy calculator on the New York State of Health: nystateofhealth.ny.gov to determine your eligibility, and to take advantage of this financial assistance.

  • [ + ]How do I get financial assistance?

    You can only get financial assistance with benefits through the New York State of Health. Our website and member services team members can advise you whether you may be eligible, but to secure your financial assistance, you must enroll through the New York State of Health: nystateofhealth.ny.gov.

    • About Consumer Operated and Oriented Plans

  • [ + ]What is a CO-OP?

    Consumer Operated and Oriented Plans, or CO-OPs, are private, member-governed health insurance companies. New CO-OPs are forming across the country as part of the Affordable Care Act, with the goal of reducing health care costs and making health insurance people accessible to millions of people. Health Republic Insurance of New York is one of 23 CO-OPs nationally.

  • [ + ]How do members participate in the CO-OP?

    There are several ways you can get involved.

    • You can invite a Health Republic Ambassador to come and speak with your organization or group about health reform changes underway and what Health Republic Insurance of New York is doing to make health care more affordable and accessible.
    • You can participate in surveys, polls and meetings that we will conduct occasionally to seek your opinion about how we can improve.
    • Eventually, you can vote for members of Health Republic of New York's Board of Directors.

  • [ + ]Is Health Republic Insurance of New York part of any other organizations?

    Health Republic is an independent organization. We have our own Board of Directors, budget and experienced leadership. We have strategic relationships with organizations that support small businesses and self-employed workers, such as Freelancers Union, and health care providers such as MagnaCare, US Script, POMCO and Healthways.

    We are not a subsidiary of Freelancers Union, and membership in Health Republic Insurance of New York provides no benefits in Freelancers Insurance Company.

  • [ + ]How do I create an HRI account?

    You can create an HRI account by completing registration.  You will need an active email account to complete this process.  

    1. At the top corner of the HRI website, click on the Register button. 

     

    1. Create a username and password for your account and provide some identifying information, as requested.  Please note the following:

     

    ​​Username: Your username will not be case-sensitive, so capitalizing the letters will not affect your ability to log in. 

    Password: Passwords are case-sensitive and must meet certain security standards, as provided by onscreen prompts.  If you forget your password, you may request a password reset by email.  

    Email: We will send important messages about your HRI account via email, including information required to complete the registration process. 

    Click Register when done. 

     

    1. Check your email for a message from us.  To complete your registration you will be asked to click on a link or copy and paste a web address in your browser.  Please follow one of these directions. 

     

    1. The link will take you to a page that indicates that you have successfully completed the registration process.  You can then log in to view your account information.  If you experience any problems, please contact Member Services at 1-888-990-5702.

     

  • [ + ]How do I log in to my HRI account?

    1. Please click the Login button at the top corner of the HRI website. 

     

    1. Enter your username and password in the appropriate boxes and click Submit to access your account.

  • [ + ]How do I link my enrollment information to my HRI account so I can view my member information, claims and/or pay my bill?

    1. First, please take note of the following:
    • ​Before 1/1/2014, you must be the primary member to complete this process.  If you are not the primary member, you will be able to link your account only after 1/1/2014.
    • We will not be able to link your account if we have not yet received the primary member's enrollment information.  Please allow 1-2 business days after enrollment to link your account. 

     

    1. Navigate to the Link Your Membership Information page.
    • The Link Your Membership Information page will appear after you log in to your HRI account for the first time.  
    • You can also access the Link Your Membership Information page via the menu at the top of the page that appears when you log in to your HRI member account. 

     

    1. Choose only ONE of two proffered ways to enter your information. 
    • Option 1: Enter the primary Member ID exactly as it appears on HRI documentation you received after your enrollment (including any capitalized letters) and the primary member's zip code. 
    • Option 2: Enter your Social Security Number and Zip Code. 

     

    1. To view and pay your bill, click Billing on the purple scroll-down menu.
